Frequently Asked Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Outsourcing logistics services can provide various benefits, such as cost savings, improved efficiency, access to specialized expertise, scalability, risk mitigation, and enhanced focus on core business activities.

Freight forwarding involves the coordination and management of shipments on behalf of clients. Freight forwarders handle tasks such as booking cargo space, arranging transportation, preparing documentation, and ensuring smooth customs clearance.

LCL (Less than Container Load) shipping consolidates multiple smaller shipments into a single container, while FCL (Full Container Load) shipping involves booking an entire container exclusively for one shipment.

Customs clearance is the process of fulfilling legal requirements and procedures to enable the import or export of goods across international borders. It involves documentation, inspections, payment of duties and taxes, and compliance with customs regulations.

Warehousing involves the storage and management of goods before they are distributed. It plays a crucial role in logistics by providing a central location for inventory management, order fulfillment, and efficient distribution.
